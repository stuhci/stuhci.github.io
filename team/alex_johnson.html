Biography

Alex Johnson is a Senior Undergraduate Student in the Department of Computer Science, working in the Human-Computer Interaction Research Lab under the supervision of Dr. Michael Chen. Alex is pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science with a minor in Psychology, expected to graduate in May 2026.

Their research focuses on mobile and wearable interfaces for health monitoring applications. Alex is currently working on their senior thesis project, which explores how smartwatch interfaces can be designed to better support mental health tracking and intervention. They are particularly interested in creating unobtrusive interfaces that can detect stress patterns and provide timely support.

Alex has completed internships at Samsung Research America and a health technology startup, where they contributed to the design and development of mobile health applications. These experiences have informed their research interests in creating technology that positively impacts users' well-being.

After graduation, Alex plans to pursue graduate studies in Human-Computer Interaction, with the goal of continuing research in health-focused interactive technologies. They are a recipient of the University Undergraduate Research Fellowship and the Computing Research Association's Outstanding Undergraduate Researcher Honorable Mention.
